# Viral Content Playbook

This is the brainstorm doc for everything designed to make School of Rizz spread — scenarios, shareable screenshot descriptors, and sharing mechanics.

## Scenario Design
Most scenarios are realistic situations guys actually face: approaching someone at a party, handling an awkward silence, asking someone out, navigating a group conversation.

But some scenarios are plausible-but-absurd, designed to be funny and shareable:
- "You ran over someone's grandma"
- "You meet someone at a feminist convention (you're a 6'4 man)"
- "Your discord kitten got a bad test grade"
- "A girl hits on you but you stay loyal to your girlfriend"
- "Someone makes fun of you for reading feminist literature"
- "Telling your girlfriend you're gay"
- "Women see you playing pickleball and start hitting on you"
- "Jeffrey Epstein invites you to his island"
- "First date with a Taylor Swift fan"

The absurd scenarios are the primary viral content engine. They get screenshotted, reacted to, argued about in comments, and stitched on TikTok.

## Shareable Output

Two shareable formats:

**Screenshot (primary viral mechanic):**
Umax-style results screen — user's face + final rizz score + category breakdown (warmth, confidence, wit, humor) + one highlight of what they did well + one soft area to improve. Designed to make people look good so they want to share it. Detailed critical feedback lives behind the paywall, not on the screenshot. One-tap share to TikTok, IG Reels, IG Stories, Snapchat, Snapchat Story, and iMessage.

**Video (secondary — influencers, UGC, marketing):**
Records the user's face + audio of both the coach agent and the character agent + live feedback overlay + rizz meter on screen. Downloadable with one-tap share to TikTok, IG Reels, IG Stories, Snapchat, Snapchat Story, and iMessage.

## Screenshot Descriptors

These appear as highlight text on the shareable screenshot. They're technically accurate social skill feedback but read as hilarious out of context — designed to get screenshotted and sent to the group chat.

### Body language / nonverbal
- "Good with his hands" — uses gestures effectively
- "Knows how to use his tongue" — articulate, good enunciation
- "Maintains eye contact" — locked in
- "Knows when to pull back" — reads the room, doesn't overcommit

### Confidence / frame
- "Doesn't finish too fast" — takes his time, doesn't rush through points
- "Stays on top" — maintains frame, leads the conversation
- "Hard to shake" — holds composure under pressure
- "Goes deep" — asks meaningful follow-up questions, not surface level
- "Knows how to take control" — steers the conversation naturally

### Humor / wit
- "Makes them moan... with laughter" — genuinely funny delivery
- "Smooth operator" — transitions between topics seamlessly
- "Dangerously charming" — charm is high enough to flag

### Warmth / connection
- "Hits the right spots" — knows exactly what to say
- "Gentle touch" — tactful, handles sensitive moments well
- "Leaves them wanting more" — ends conversations at the peak

### Overall titles
- "Certified lover boy" — top tier overall score
- "Risk to the public" — dangerously high rizz
- "NPC killer" — breaks people out of autopilot conversations
- "Heartbreak hazard" — too smooth for their own good
