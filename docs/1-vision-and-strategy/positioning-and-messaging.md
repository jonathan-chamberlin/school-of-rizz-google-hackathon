# Positioning & Messaging Doc

## Category
AI rizz coach — practice talking to people, get scored, get better.

## One-Liner
"Get your rizz score."

## Core Messaging Pillars
1. You can't get better at talking to people by watching videos. You need reps.
2. School of Rizz is the only app that watches you talk and tells you what's working.
3. Your rizz score is real — it's based on how you actually perform, not a quiz.
4. The scenarios are fun enough to share, useful enough to actually improve.

## Tone of Voice
**Casual confidence.** Like your funniest friend who's also inexplicably good with people.

Specifics:
- **Direct, no-BS** — says what it means, doesn't hedge or over-explain.
- **Meme-literate** — understands internet culture natively. "Discord kitten" is a valid scenario title.
- **Slightly provocative** — edges up to "is this allowed?" without being offensive. The kind of thing that makes people comment and argue (= algorithm fuel).
- **Competitive** — "you got a 6.2, your friend got an 8.1" energy.
- **Zero cringe-awareness** — doesn't apologize for itself. Doesn't say "this is just for fun." Just gives you a number.
- **Never preachy** — not here to fix you. Here to make you better at something you already want to be better at.

## Language to Avoid
- Therapy language ("healing journey", "safe space", "vulnerability", "growth mindset")
- Corporate/premium language ("curated experience", "personalized journey", "unlock your potential")
- Vague motivational fluff ("you got this!", "believe in yourself", "be your best self")
- Anything that makes a 21-year-old guy cringe when he sees it on his screen

## Language to Use
- Scores, ratings, metrics (rizz score, confidence rating, warmth score)
- Competition framing (beat your friends, climb the leaderboard, top 10%)
- Internet-native slang where appropriate (rizz, W, L, NPC, goated)
- Short, punchy copy. If a sentence is longer than 10 words, it's probably too long.

## Emotional Promise
You already watch the content. School of Rizz lets you actually practice.

## Virality Strategy

### Primary: The Screenshot
Umax-style results screen after each session:
- User's face
- Rizz score (prominent, large number)
- Category breakdown: warmth, confidence, wit, humor
- One highlight of what they did well (makes them want to share)
- One soft area to improve (so it feels real, not fake)
- School of Rizz branding/watermark

One-tap share buttons: TikTok, IG Reels, IG Stories, Snapchat, Snapchat Story, iMessage.

This is designed to be **brag-friendly**. People share wins. The screenshot should make them look good. Detailed critical feedback lives in the app behind the paywall, not on the shareable screen.

### Secondary: The Video
Full recording of the roleplay session:
- User's face (front camera)
- Audio of the AI character + coach
- Live feedback overlay appearing in real time
- Rizz meter visible on screen throughout
- Downloadable with one-tap share buttons: TikTok, IG Reels, IG Stories, Snapchat, Snapchat Story, iMessage

Video sharing targets: influencers, UGC creators, and the School of Rizz team for organic marketing. Most regular users will share the screenshot; creators will share the video.

### Why It Spreads
- **Absurd scenarios** get screenshotted and sent to group chats, argued about in comments, stitched on TikTok
- **Rizz scores** are inherently competitive — "I got higher than you"
- **The screenshot** makes people look good if they scored well
- **The video** is reaction-bait content for creators
- **Micro-influencer seeding** — pay small TikTok creators $50 to do a funny scenario on camera. Two posts can drive hundreds of thousands of downloads (App Mafia playbook).

### Free Hook (Conversion Funnel)
Curiosity → Entertainment → Value → Subscription

1. **Free:** One roleplay with live coaching (~3 min) → rizz score + shareable screenshot
2. **Paywall:** Detailed feedback breakdown, unlimited sessions, all scenarios, progress tracking
