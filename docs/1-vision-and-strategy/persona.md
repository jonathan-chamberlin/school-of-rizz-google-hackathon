# Target User

## Demographics
- Male, 18-28.
- College student or early career.
- Lives in or near a city, or has at least a small social circle to test skills with.
- Has a smartphone and is active on TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and/or Snapchat.

## Psychographics
- Self-improvement oriented. Watches content from creators like Hamza, Michael Sartain, Owen Cook, Playing with Fire.
- Consumes dating content, manosphere content, and general self-improvement content.
- Competitive — wants to be better than his friends, wants measurable progress.
- Analytical — responds to scores, metrics, and clear feedback over vague encouragement.
- Performance-oriented — treats social skills like a skill to grind, not a feeling to explore.

## Pain Points
- Knows he could be better with girls, in groups, or socially in general — but doesn't know how to practice.
- Has consumed tons of content (YouTube, podcasts, courses) but still freezes in the moment.
- Cold approaching feels impossible without a way to practice first.
- No honest feedback loop — friends won't tell you you're awkward.

## What He's Already Tried
- YouTube videos on social skills, dating, confidence — gets motivated but nothing changes.
- Cold approaching — high anxiety, inconsistent results, no feedback on what went wrong.
- Maybe a dating coach or course — expensive, often generic advice.

## Content He Already Consumes
- Hamza (masculinity, self-improvement, discipline)
- Michael Sartain (social circle game, high-status networking)
- Owen Cook / RSD (infield pickup, social dynamics)
- Playing with Fire (dating, social dynamics, real-world scenarios)
- Looksmaxxing content (Umax, jawline exercises, style)
- General manosphere and red pill adjacent content

## Objections / Skepticism
- "Is talking to an AI actually going to help me in real life?"
- "Is this cringe? Will my friends roast me for using this?"
- "Can AI actually tell if I'm being awkward or confident?"
- "The scenarios won't feel real enough to matter."
- "I've tried apps before and they don't stick."

## Why He'll Share It
- The rizz score is inherently competitive — "I got higher than you."
- The absurd scenarios are funny enough to screenshot and send to the group chat.
- The Umax-style results screen makes him look good if he scored well.
- Video recordings of funny scenarios are reaction-bait content.
